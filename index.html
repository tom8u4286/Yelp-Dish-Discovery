ABSTRACT

Consumers nowadays are bombarded with all sorts of food reviews, but in fact they require a more e cient recommendation service. In light of this, it would be of great convenience if there is a system to provide analysis on the reveiws and return a list of recommended delicacies by others users. Therefore, in this paper, we propose a recommendation system for dishes, empirically showing that it is feasible to extract sentiment and opinions to a dish out of its context.


INTRODUCTION

With the growth of social media, corporations, such as Yelp, have accumulated a great number of user-generated reviews. In light of this, researches have been conducted with a view to nding the crucial information hidden in the context, aiming to nd the user's evaluation pertaining a certain service of an institution. Though a number of works have been proposed on the accurate sentiment interpretation towards reviews and recommendation, few has speci cally focus on dish-level analysis. Therefore, in this paper, we propose an approach and a system providing dish recommendation. We collect our data from Yelp Dataset Challenge, and after preprocessing, we transform all words in the corpus into high-dimension vectors, calculating the Euclidean distances among dishes and all the positive sentiment words. The results of experiments show that dishes of lower Euclidean distances with the positive sentiment words are the most recommended dishes by the users.
